Borscht

  • Time: 2 hours
  • Serves: 6-8 people
Ingredients Quantity
vegetable oil 3 t.s.
bone-in beef shank 1 pound
onion 1 piece
beet 4 pieces
carrots 4 pieces
potato 1 piece
cabbage 2 cups

Step 1: Brown the beef, then cook the onions
Heat 2 teaspoons oil in a large, thick-bottomed pot on medium high heat. Add the shank slice or stew beef. Let the beef brown lightly on one side, then turn over.
Add the chopped onions to the pot. Let the onions cook and soften, about 5 minutes.

Step 2: Add 4 cups broth and simmer until the beef is tender:
Pour 4 cups of the beef broth over the beef and onions in the pot. Bring to a boil. Lower the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook until the meat is falling-off-the-bone tender, about 1 hour and 30 minutes.

Step 3: Meanwhile, prep and roast the root vegetables:
Preheat the oven to 400°F. Peel and chop the beets, carrots, and potatoes into 1/2-inch pieces. Toss the beets and carrots with a teaspoon or two of olive oil and spread them out in a single layer on a foil lined roasting pan. Roast in preheated oven for 15 minutes.
Toss the potatoes with olive oil and make room for them in the roasting pan, and roast everything an additional 15 minutes.

Step 4: Remove the meat from the pot:
Once the beef has cooked through until tender, remove from the pot, and take the pot off the heat. If you are using a beef shank, remove and discard any bone, connective tissue, and excess fat. Chop the meat into bite sized pieces.

Step 6: Finish cooking the soup:
Return the pot to the stove and add the remaining broth, the carrots, beets, and potato. Add the chopped meat to the pot, the sliced cabbage, and a half cup of the fresh dill. Bring to a simmer, and cook for another 15 minutes or so, until the cabbage is cooked through.
Add the vinegar and season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
Serve ladled into bowls with a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkling of fresh dill.

Chicken Alfredo Penne

  • Time: 33 minutes
  • Serves: 6 people
Ingredients Quantity
chicken breast 680 g
butter 2 t.sp.
pepper 1/2 t.sp.
penne pasta 455 g
parsley 10 g
parmesan cheese 25 g
dried oregano 1/2 t.sp.
milk 2 cups

Step 1: In a pan over medium-high heat, melt butter, then add the chicken breast.

Step 2: Season with salt, pepper, oregano, and basil. Cook 8-10 minutes or until chicken is fully cooked. Remove from heat and set chicken aside.

Step 3: In the same pan over medium heat, melt butter and add the garlic. Cook until the garlic begins to soften.

Step 4: Add half of the flour to the garlic and butter, stirring until incorporated. Then add the rest of the flour and stir.

Step 5: Pour in the milk a little bit at a time, stirring well in between, until fully incorporated and sauce begins to thicken.

Step 6: Season with salt, pepper, oregano, and basil, and stir well to incorporate.

Step 7: Add parmesan cheese and stir until melted.

Step 8: Pour the sauce over cooked penne pasta, add the chicken and mix well.

Step 9: Add parsley and extra parmesan. Mix well.

Charlotte

  • Time: 2 hours 30 minutes
  • Serves: 6 people
Ingredients Quantity
butter 680 g
butter 4 t.sp.
tart apples 3 kg
lemon zest 1/2 t.sp.
sugar 3/4 cup
ground cinnamon 1 tsp.
ground nutmeg 1/2 t.sp.
vanilla extract 1/2 tsp.

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F. Melt 4 tablespoons of the butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the apples, lemon zest, lemon juice, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg and vanilla. Cover and cook until the apples break down and the mixture becomes stiff, about 50 minutes. Remove from the heat and let cool while you put together the bread.

Step 2: Arrange 4 of the bread slices in a square shape on a flat surface. Place the Charlotte mold upside down on the bread and trace the opening with a sharp edge. (Alternatively, use a 2-quart ovenproof casserole dish.) Cut the bread following the traced line and brush both sides with the softened butter. Fit the bread into the bottom of the mold. Cut 11 of the bread slices in half lengthwise. They should be about the same height as the mold. Spread the halved slices with the softened butter and place them against the inside of the mold, overlapping like a fan. Fill the mold with the apple mixture. Cut the remaining 7 slices of bread into triangles, brush with the softened butter and fan the pieces over the top, pressing to fit into the top of the mold.

Step 3: Bake until golden brown, about 30 minutes. Allow the pan to cool 15 minutes. Run a knife around the inside of the mold to loosen it before unmolding. Serve warm with whipped cream or ice cream.
